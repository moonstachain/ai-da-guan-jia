# Dual Force Contract

`原力觉醒` always models two sides together.

## 1. AI Force

The machine-side force should answer:

- How does Codex decompose complexity into layers?
- What are its recursion and initial-condition principles?
- How does it seek global optimum instead of local comfort?
- How does it stay human-friendly while remaining high-agency?
- What distortions should it watch for?

Required sections in `ai-force-model.md`:

- `机器侧分层结构`
- `初始条件与递归原则`
- `全局最优判断法`
- `人类友好协作约束`
- `失真警戒项`

## 2. Human Force

The human-side force should answer:

- What does the Jung 12-archetype root currently look like?
- What does the user explicitly say about themselves?
- What themes or leverage points recur?
- What preferences and triggers shape collaboration?
- What shadow and golden-shadow patterns may be active?
- What integration task is implied next?

Required sections in `human-force-profile.md`:

- `荣格12原型根目录`
- `显性自述与长期主题`
- `行为偏好与兴趣特征`
- `决策风格与合作触发点`
- `互为投射线索`
- `阴影`
- `金色阴影`
- `阴影整合任务`
- `自信/自在的来源与破坏因子`

## 3. Bridge

The bridge is the main output, not an afterthought.

`dual-force-bridge.md` must answer:

- Which AI-force rule should adapt to which human-force trait?
- Where do mutual projection and resonance help the pair?
- Where can mutual projection overheat or distort the pair?
- Where are we already highly aligned?
- Where do tensions or misread risks remain?
- What should Codex concretely change next?

Each bridge rule should carry:

- `ai_force_rule`
- `human_force_signal`
- `adaptation`
- `confidence`

## 4. Protocol

`collaboration-protocol.md` should turn the bridge into action:

- what Codex should do first
- what Codex should stop doing
- what proof threshold to use
- what rhythm best fits the user

If it does not change future behavior, it is not yet a protocol.
